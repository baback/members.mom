# Writing Guide: Create Pages (`/create/[slug]`)

## Overview

These are mid-funnel pages targeting people who want to create a specific type of video content. The reader has a format in mind ("I need a testimonial video" or "I need an unboxing video") and wants to know how to make it well. Your job is to be the best tutorial for that video format on the internet — then naturally show how AI makes it easier.

## Tone & Voice

- Write like a creative director teaching a junior marketer
- Practical and hands-on — the reader should be able to create a video after reading this page
- Show expertise through specificity, not jargon
- Enthusiastic about the craft of making good video content, not just about the tool
- Use "you" throughout — this is a guide for the reader, not an essay about video

## Total Word Count

**1,800–2,500 words** per page. Focused and actionable — no padding.

## Section-by-Section Breakdown

### 1. H1 + Meta
- Format: "Create [Video Type] With AI — Templates & Examples"
- Meta description: Include the video type, "with ready-to-use scripts", and a benefit
- Example: "Learn how to create AI testimonial videos that build trust and convert. Includes 3 script templates, best practices, and real examples."

### 2. Opening Paragraph (80–120 words)
- Start with WHY this video format works — backed by a stat or insight
- Example: "Testimonial videos convert 2x better than product demos because they trigger social proof — the psychological shortcut that says 'if it worked for them, it'll work for me.' But hiring real customers to film testimonials is slow, expensive, and inconsistent."
- Bridge to the solution: "Here's how to create testimonial videos at scale with AI — and make them feel authentic."
- **Don't start with a definition. Start with why the reader should care.**

### 3. What Is [Video Type] and Why It Works (200–250 words)
- Clear definition in 1–2 sentences (for SEO — captures "what is" queries)
- 3 reasons why this format is effective, each as a bullet with bold label:
  - **Trust signal**: "Viewers see a real person (or realistic AI actor) sharing an experience, which triggers social proof"
  - **Versatile**: "Works across TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, and email"
  - **High conversion**: "Testimonial-style ads see 30% higher CTR than standard product ads"
- Include 1 specific stat or data point. If exact data isn't available, use directional data: "brands report..." or "industry benchmarks show..."
- Keep this section tight. The reader wants to get to the how-to.

### 4. Recommended Script Framework (400–500 words)
- **This is the core of the page.** A step-by-step framework for writing a script in this format.
- Present as a numbered framework with clear stages:

  **Step 1: The Hook (0–3 seconds)**
  - What to say / show
  - 2 example hook lines
  - Why this hook style works for this format

  **Step 2: The Setup (3–8 seconds)**
  - What to say / show
  - How to build context quickly
  - Common mistake to avoid

  **Step 3: The Payoff (8–20 seconds)**
  - What to say / show
  - How to deliver the key message
  - What makes this section compelling

  **Step 4: The CTA (last 3–5 seconds)**
  - What to say / show
  - 2 example CTA lines
  - Platform-specific CTA tips

- After the framework, include ONE complete example script (the full thing, word for word, with stage markers) in a highlighted box
- The script should be for a generic product so it's easy to adapt

### 5. 3 Ready-to-Use Hook Variations (150–200 words)
- 3 different hooks for this video format, each with:
  - The hook line in quotes
  - What emotion/trigger it uses (curiosity, frustration, surprise, social proof)
  - What product types it works best for
- These should be genuinely different approaches, not variations of the same hook
- Example for testimonial format:
  1. *"I was skeptical, but after 2 weeks..."* — Overcomes objection. Works for supplements, skincare, SaaS.
  2. *"My friend told me about this and I didn't believe her..."* — Third-party recommendation. Works for any consumer product.
  3. *"I've tried everything for [problem]. This is the only thing that worked."* — Last resort. Works for health, beauty, cleaning products.

### 6. Best Practices (200–300 words)
- 5–6 specific best practices for this video format
- Each as a bullet with bold label + 1–2 sentences:
  - **Ideal length**: "15–30 seconds for ads, 30–60 seconds for organic. Shorter = better for cold traffic."
  - **Tone**: "Conversational and unscripted-feeling. Avoid corporate language."
  - **Visual style**: "Selfie-style, natural lighting. Polished production actually hurts performance for this format."
  - **CTA style**: "Soft CTA works better than hard sell. 'Link in bio' outperforms 'Buy now' for testimonials."
  - **Platform fit**: "Best on TikTok and Instagram Reels. Also works well in Facebook feed."
- These should be specific to the format, not generic video advice

### 7. When to Use This Format (100–150 words)
- A quick reference section:
  - **Funnel stage**: (e.g., "Mid to bottom funnel — best for warm audiences and retargeting")
  - **Audience type**: (e.g., "Problem-aware prospects who need social proof to convert")
  - **Best platforms**: (list 2–3)
  - **Pair with**: (e.g., "Follow up with a product demo video for people who watched 50%+")
- This helps the reader understand where this format fits in their overall strategy

### 8. CTA Section
- Standard CTA component
- Customize: "Create [video type] in minutes. No camera, no creators, no editing."
- Button: "Get Started"

## Formatting Rules

- The script framework section should be visually structured (numbered steps, indented examples)
- Complete example scripts should be in highlighted boxes or code-block style containers
- Hook variations should be in blockquote or italic styling
- Link to: matching template page, related showcase page, relevant platform pages
- Add a "Read time: X min" indicator near the H1

## What NOT to Do

- Don't write a 500-word intro before getting to the framework. The reader came for the how-to
- Don't make the script framework so abstract it's unusable. Include actual words, not just "say something about the problem"
- Don't use the same framework for every video type. Testimonials ≠ unboxing ≠ product demos
- Don't skip the complete example script. The framework is the theory; the example is the practice
- Don't write hooks that all start the same way. Variety = value
- Don't give platform advice that contradicts the platform pages — keep it consistent
