# Writing Guide: Industry Pages (`/industries/[slug]`)

## Overview

These are mid-funnel pages targeting marketers in a specific industry who are searching for video marketing solutions. The reader knows they need video content but hasn't decided on a tool yet. Your job is to be the definitive resource for video marketing in their industry — so good they bookmark it.

## Tone & Voice

- Write like an industry consultant, not a tool vendor
- Show deep understanding of the industry's specific challenges (not generic marketing advice)
- Use industry-specific language naturally (e.g., "AOV" for e-commerce, "chair time" for dental, "RevPAR" for hotels)
- Be practical above all — every paragraph should contain something actionable
- Reference real patterns, real numbers, real scenarios from that industry

## Total Word Count

**2,000–3,000 words** per page. Enough to be a genuine guide, not so long it becomes a textbook.

## Section-by-Section Breakdown

### 1. H1 + Meta
- Format: "AI UGC Videos for [Industry]: [Benefit-Driven Subtitle]"
- Meta description: Include industry name, a specific benefit ("cut production costs by 50x"), and a hook ("with scripts and examples")
- Example: "AI UGC Videos for Skincare Brands: Cut Content Costs by 50x While Scaling Ad Creative"

### 2. Opening Paragraph (100–150 words)
- Start with a specific industry pain point related to video content
- NOT "Video marketing is important for [industry]" — that's obvious and boring
- Instead: "The average skincare brand spends $2,400/month on UGC creators for 8–10 videos. Half of those videos underperform. You're burning cash on content that doesn't convert, and you can't test fast enough to find what works."
- End with what this guide covers
- **The opening must make the reader feel understood.** They should think "this person gets my business."

### 3. Why [Industry] Needs a Different Video Approach (200–300 words)
- 3–4 specific challenges this industry faces with video content
- Each challenge should be a subheading (H3) with 2–3 sentences
- Examples for skincare: "Demonstrating texture and application on camera", "Keeping up with TikTok trends weekly", "Diverse skin types require diverse creators"
- This section proves you understand their world before pitching anything

### 4. Video Formats That Work for [Industry] (400–500 words)
- 4 recommended video formats, each with:
  - **Format name** (H3)
  - **Why it works for this industry** (2–3 sentences)
  - **Ideal length** (e.g., "15–30 seconds")
  - **Best platform** (e.g., "TikTok, Instagram Reels")
  - **Example script outline** (3–4 bullet points showing the flow: hook → body → CTA)
- Formats should be industry-specific, not generic. "Unboxing" works for e-commerce but not dental. "Before/After" works for skincare but not SaaS.
- **This is the most valuable section.** Make it genuinely useful — someone should be able to take these formats and create videos immediately.

### 5. Stats Section (150–200 words)
- 3 key metrics presented in a visually prominent way (large numbers, highlight boxes)
- Format per stat:
  - **The number** (big, bold): e.g., "50x"
  - **What it means**: "cheaper than hiring UGC creators"
  - **Context**: 1 sentence explaining the comparison
- Stats should be relevant to the industry, not generic video stats
- If exact industry stats aren't available, use the closest relevant data and frame it honestly: "E-commerce brands using UGC video ads see..." rather than making up industry-specific numbers

### 6. 5 Example Hooks for [Industry] (200–250 words)
- 5 ready-to-use opening lines for video ads in this industry
- Each hook should be:
  - In quotes, as if someone is saying it on camera
  - Followed by (1 sentence) explaining why it works
  - Tagged with the format it fits: [Testimonial], [Problem-Solution], [Curiosity], etc.
- Example: *"I spent $200 on serums last year and my skin looked the same..."* — Opens with relatable frustration. Works for problem-solution format. [Problem-Solution]
- **These hooks should feel like they were written by a copywriter, not generated in bulk**

### 7. How Brands in [Industry] Use ReUGC (300–400 words)
- 3 mini use-case stories (not case studies — hypothetical but realistic scenarios)
- Each story: 80–120 words
- Structure per story:
  - **Brand type** (e.g., "A DTC skincare brand doing $50K/month")
  - **Their challenge** (1 sentence)
  - **What they did** (2 sentences — specific: "Created 30 testimonial-style videos in 3 languages")
  - **The result** (1 sentence with a number: "Cut CPA by 40% in the first month")
- These should feel realistic and specific, not like marketing fluff
- Vary the brand sizes: 1 small, 1 mid, 1 larger

### 8. FAQ (4 questions, 60–100 words each)
- Questions must be industry-specific:
  - "How much do [industry] brands typically spend on video content?"
  - "What video length works best for [industry] ads?"
  - "Can AI actors look authentic for [industry] products?"
  - "How many video ads should a [industry] brand test per month?"
- Answers: direct answer first, then context
- Use FAQ schema markup

### 9. CTA Section
- Standard CTA component
- Customize the headline to the industry: "Stop overpaying for [industry] video content."
- Button: "Get Started"

## Formatting Rules

- Use H2 for main sections, H3 for subsections
- Script outlines should be in ordered lists (1. Hook, 2. Problem, 3. Solution, 4. CTA)
- Stats section should use a 3-column grid layout on desktop
- Hook examples should be in blockquote or italic styling to stand out
- Link to related pages: matching template page, showcase page, platform pages
- Add "Last updated: [month] 2026" below H1

## What NOT to Do

- Don't write generic advice that applies to every industry. If you could swap the industry name and the content still works, it's too generic
- Don't make up specific case study numbers and attribute them to real brands
- Don't use the same 4 video formats for every industry — tailor them
- Don't skip the hooks section or make them generic. This is what makes the page worth bookmarking
- Don't write "video is important for [industry]" — the reader already knows that, that's why they're here
- Don't use jargon from the wrong industry
