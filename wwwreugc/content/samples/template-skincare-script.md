## Script 1: The Skincare Testimonial

**Best for:** Building trust with cold audiences. Retargeting site visitors who haven't purchased.
**Duration:** 20–25 seconds

```
[HOOK — 0-3s]
"Okay so I've been using this serum for about 3 weeks 
now and I have to be honest with you..."
[Direct to camera, natural lighting, no makeup visible]

[SETUP — 3-9s]
"I've tried literally everything for my dark spots. 
The $60 vitamin C serums, the retinol, even a chemical 
peel — and nothing actually made a visible difference. 
I was about to just accept it."

[PAYOFF — 9-19s]
"But look at my skin right now. These spots right here? 
They're like 80% lighter. I'm not wearing any foundation 
in this video. This is just... my skin now."
[Point to areas, show skin close-up, hold product]

[CTA — 19-23s]
"I'm already on my second bottle. If you've been 
struggling with dark spots, just try it. Link in bio."
[Hold up product, point down toward bio]
```

**Customization notes:**
- Swap "dark spots" for your customer's #1 skin concern (acne, texture, dryness, redness, fine lines)
- Replace "3 weeks" with a realistic timeline for your product — don't overpromise. If results take 6 weeks, say 6 weeks. Honesty converts better than hype.
- The "I have to be honest" hook creates an open loop — keep it. It works because the viewer doesn't know if the review will be positive or negative.
- "I'm not wearing any foundation" is a powerful proof statement for skincare. Adapt it to your product: "I'm not using any filter" or "This is straight out of the shower."

## Script 2: The Skincare Problem-Solution

**Best for:** Demonstrating product benefits. Mid-funnel audiences who know they have a problem but haven't found a solution.
**Duration:** 18–25 seconds

```
[HOOK — 0-3s]
"If your skin looks good in the morning but turns into 
an oil slick by 2pm, listen up."
[Direct to camera, relatable frustrated expression]

[PROBLEM — 3-8s]
"I used to blot my face 3 times a day. I tried mattifying 
primers, setting sprays, even powder — and by lunch my 
makeup was literally sliding off my face."

[SOLUTION — 8-17s]
"Then someone told me the problem wasn't oil control — 
it was hydration. My skin was overproducing oil because 
it was dehydrated. I switched to this moisturizer and 
within a week, the oil thing just... stopped."
[Show product, apply a small amount]

[CTA — 17-22s]
"If you're fighting oil, stop trying to control it and 
start hydrating. This is the one that worked for me. 
Link in bio."
```

**Customization notes:**
- The "if you [specific problem], listen up" hook is a qualifier — it immediately tells the right audience "this is for you" and the wrong audience "skip this." Qualified viewers watch longer.
- The problem section should describe the experience, not just name the condition. "My makeup was sliding off my face" is more relatable than "I had oily skin."
- The solution includes an insight ("the problem wasn't oil control — it was hydration") which makes the viewer feel like they learned something. Educational hooks build trust.
- Adapt the specific problem/insight/solution to your product. The structure works for any skincare concern — acne (the problem isn't dirt, it's bacteria), aging (the problem isn't wrinkles, it's collagen loss), etc.

## Script 3: The Skincare Hook-Demo-CTA

**Best for:** Product-focused ads. Showing the product in action. Cold audiences on TikTok and Reels.
**Duration:** 13–17 seconds

```
[HOOK — 0-2s]
"Stop scrolling if you have textured skin"
[TEXT ON SCREEN: same as voiceover]
[Hold product up, start moving immediately]

[DEMO — 2-10s]
[Show product being dispensed — the texture, the color]
"One pump. Spread it on like this."
[Apply to skin in real-time]
"Feel how it just melts in? No residue, no stickiness."
[Show skin absorbing product]

[RESULT — 10-13s]
[Close-up of skin after application]
"Instant blur. Every single time."

[CTA — 13-16s]
[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Link in bio 👇"]
"You need this in your routine."
```

**Customization notes:**
- "Stop scrolling if you..." is one of the highest-performing hook formats on TikTok. Replace "textured skin" with whatever concern your product addresses.
- The demo section should be FAST. No pauses, no long explanations. Show the product being used in real-time with quick cuts.
- "Instant blur" is a specific, visual result. Replace with your product's immediate visible effect: "instant glow", "instant hydration", "instant calm" (for redness).
- This script is shorter than the others by design. Demo-style ads work best under 17 seconds — the viewer gets the point quickly and either clicks or moves on.

## 5 Hook Variations for Skincare

1. *"I can't believe I'm showing you my skin without makeup right now..."* (vulnerability) — Creates intimacy and trust. The viewer feels like they're seeing something real. Great for before/after reveals and testimonials.

2. *"My dermatologist is going to hate me for saying this..."* (controversy) — Implies insider knowledge that goes against professional advice. Extremely high thumb-stop rate. Use for products with unconventional ingredients or approaches.

3. *"POV: you finally find a serum that doesn't break you out"* (relatability) — Native TikTok format. The "POV:" prefix signals organic content. Feels like a creator post, not an ad. Best for TikTok and Reels.

4. *"$14 and it outperforms my $80 serum"* (value comparison) — Price anchoring. The viewer immediately thinks "wait, really?" Works for affordable products competing against premium brands. Replace prices with your actual numbers.

5. *"3 weeks ago my skin looked completely different"* (transformation) — Sets up a before/after story. The viewer wants to see the change. Works for any product with visible results over time.

## Tips for Customizing These Scripts

- Replace product details but keep the emotional structure intact — the hook-setup-payoff-CTA flow is what converts, not the specific words
- Match the AI actor's demographic to your target customer. A 20-year-old promoting anti-aging serum won't convert with a 45-year-old audience
- Test each script with 3 different hooks before judging which script "works." Often the hook is the variable, not the body
- Shorter is almost always better for cold traffic. If you're running to people who've never heard of your brand, cut anything that doesn't earn its seconds
- Add captions — 85% of social video is watched without sound initially. Captions also increase watch time by 15–25%
