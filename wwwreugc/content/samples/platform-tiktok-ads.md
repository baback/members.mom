| Spec | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Aspect ratio** | **9:16 (recommended)**, 1:1, 16:9 supported |
| **Resolution** | Minimum 540x960px. **Recommended: 1080x1920px** |

| **Max file size** | 500 MB |

## Top 3 Performing Ad Formats on TikTok

### 1. The Native Testimonial

TikTok's algorithm treats this format almost like organic content because it mirrors how real creators post. Selfie-style framing, natural lighting, casual speech. The algorithm can't easily distinguish it from a regular TikTok, which means better delivery and lower CPMs.

**Structure:**
- **0–2s:** Hook — emotional or surprising opening line, delivered directly to camera
- **2–8s:** Story — the problem, the discovery, the experience (keep it conversational)
- **8–15s:** Result — what changed, how it feels now
- **15–18s:** CTA — casual, not salesy ("link in bio, trust me")

**Ideal length:** 15–21 seconds
**First frame:** Close-up of face, natural lighting, no branding visible. Should look like a FYP video, not an ad.

### 2. The Stitch-Style Reaction

This format mimics TikTok's native "Stitch" feature — it looks like someone is reacting to or building on another video. It triggers curiosity because the viewer feels like they're watching a conversation, not an ad. High thumb-stop rate because the format is familiar.

**Structure:**
- **0–2s:** "I saw this [product/claim/trend] and had to try it..."
- **2–6s:** Show the product or reference what they're reacting to
- **6–15s:** Their honest reaction/experience — surprise, skepticism turned to belief
- **15–18s:** CTA — "okay I'm convinced, link in bio"

**Ideal length:** 15–20 seconds
**First frame:** Person looking at phone or off-camera (as if watching another video). Creates the "stitch" illusion.

### 3. The Rapid-Fire Demo

TikTok rewards fast pacing. This format shows the product in action with quick cuts, text overlays, and a punchy voiceover. No slow intros, no long explanations — just show what it does in under 15 seconds. Works especially well for products with a visible result (skincare, cleaning, food).

**Structure:**
- **0–1s:** Bold text hook on screen + voiceover: "Watch this"
- **1–8s:** Product demo with 3–4 quick cuts showing different angles/uses
- **8–12s:** Result shot — the clean surface, the glowing skin, the finished dish
- **12–15s:** CTA text overlay + voiceover

**Ideal length:** 12–15 seconds
**First frame:** Product in hand or bold text on screen. Movement in the first frame is critical — static first frames get scrolled past.

## 3 Ready-to-Use TikTok Ad Scripts

### Script 1: The Honest Testimonial
**Best for:** Any consumer product, cold and warm audiences
**Duration:** 18–22 seconds

```
[HOOK — 0-2s]
"Okay I need to be honest about something..."
[Look directly at camera, slightly hesitant expression]

[STORY — 2-10s]
"I bought this because TikTok made me do it and I was 
fully expecting to return it. But it's been 2 weeks and 
I literally can't stop using it."
[Hold product casually, gesture naturally]

[RESULT — 10-16s]
"Look at this. [Show result — skin, clean surface, etc.] 
I've tried so many things and this is the first one that 
actually did what it said it would."

[CTA — 16-20s]
"I'm not even joking, go get this. Link in bio."
[Point down toward bio link area]
```

**Why this works on TikTok:** The "I need to be honest" hook creates tension — is this going to be positive or negative? The viewer has to keep watching. The "TikTok made me do it" reference makes it feel native. The casual language ("literally", "I'm not even joking") matches how real TikTok creators talk.

**Customization:** Replace the product reference and result with yours. Keep the hesitant-to-convinced emotional arc — it's the structure that converts, not the specific words.

### Script 2: The Controversy Hook
**Best for:** Products that challenge conventional wisdom, health/beauty/cleaning
**Duration:** 15–18 seconds

```
[HOOK — 0-2s]
"Your [product category] is probably making things worse. 
Here's why."
[Serious expression, direct to camera]

[EXPLAIN — 2-8s]
"Most [product category] products use [common ingredient] 
which actually [negative effect]. I switched to [product] 
because it uses [better ingredient] instead."

[PROOF — 8-13s]
"After [timeframe], [specific result]. Not even kidding."
[Show product or result]

[CTA — 13-16s]
"Check yours. Then check this one. Link in bio."
[Hold up product]
```

**Why this works on TikTok:** Controversy hooks have the highest thumb-stop rate on TikTok — they trigger "wait, am I doing something wrong?" which is impossible to scroll past. The educational angle ("here's why") positions the ad as helpful content, not a sales pitch.

**Customization:** The controversy must be real and defensible — don't make false claims. The "check yours, then check this one" CTA is powerful because it creates a comparison moment.

### Script 3: The Speed Demo
**Best for:** Products with visible results, e-commerce, cold audiences
**Duration:** 12–15 seconds

```
[HOOK — 0-1s]
[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Watch this"]
[Product in hand, movement starts immediately]

[DEMO — 1-8s]
[Quick cuts showing product in use]
[Voiceover: "One pump. Spread it on. Wait 30 seconds."]
[Each step = 2 seconds max, keep cutting]

[RESULT — 8-11s]
[Show the result — clean, glowing, transformed]
[Voiceover: "Every. Single. Time."]

[CTA — 11-14s]
[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Link in bio 👇"]
[Voiceover: "You need this."]
```

**Why this works on TikTok:** No talking head, no long intro — just product in action. TikTok's algorithm favors videos with high completion rates, and short demos get watched to the end (and rewatched). The text-on-screen hook works because it's how native TikTok content looks.

**Customization:** This script is a framework — the specific demo steps depend on your product. The key is speed: never spend more than 2 seconds on any single shot.

## Creative Testing Tips for TikTok

**Test hooks independently, not whole videos.** Write one solid body + CTA, then create 5 versions with different hooks. TikTok's algorithm decides whether to show your ad based on the first 0.5–1 second. If your hook doesn't stop the scroll, the rest of the video doesn't matter. Test hooks first, optimize everything else second.

**Spend $30–$50 per variation before judging.** TikTok needs data to optimize. Killing an ad after $10 in spend doesn't give the algorithm enough signal. Run each variation for at least $30 (ideally $50) before deciding winners and losers. At $5–$10 CPM, that's 3,000–10,000 impressions — enough to see a pattern.

**Use Spark Ads for your winners.** Once you find a creative that works, run it as a Spark Ad (boosted from a TikTok account) rather than a standard In-Feed Ad. Spark Ads get 30–40% higher engagement because they look like organic posts with social proof (likes, comments, shares visible).

**Refresh creatives every 7–14 days.** TikTok creative fatigue hits faster than any other platform. A winning ad today will see declining performance within 1–2 weeks. Always have 3–5 new creatives in the pipeline ready to replace fatigued ones. This is where AI batch generation pays for itself.

**Test vertical (9:16) only.** Square (1:1) and horizontal (16:9) technically work on TikTok, but they waste screen real estate and signal "this wasn't made for TikTok." Always create in 9:16. No exceptions.

## Do's and Don'ts

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Use trending sounds (even at low volume under voiceover) — it signals native content to the algorithm | Don't put your logo in the first 3 seconds — it screams "ad" and kills watch time |
| Start with movement in the first frame — static openings get scrolled past | Don't use horizontal video cropped to vertical — black bars = instant skip |
| Add burned-in captions — they increase watch time by 15–25% | Don't use corporate language ("We're excited to announce...") — it doesn't belong on TikTok |
| Film (or generate) in selfie-style framing — it matches how creators post | Don't start with the product name — start with a hook that creates curiosity |
| Use TikTok's native fonts for text overlays when possible | Don't use the same creative for more than 2 weeks — refresh constantly |
| Keep the first 2 seconds surprising, emotional, or visually striking | Don't use stock music — it sounds generic. Use trending sounds or original audio |
