# Writing Guide: Showcase Pages (`/showcase/[slug]`)

## Overview

These are mid-funnel inspiration pages for people searching "[niche] UGC ad examples" or "best [platform] video ads." The reader wants to SEE what good looks like — they're looking for examples, patterns, and ideas they can steal. This page type is visual-first. The video cards ARE the content; the writing supports and explains them.

## Tone & Voice

- Write like a creative strategist doing an ad breakdown on Twitter/X
- Analytical but accessible — explain WHY something works, not just THAT it works
- Enthusiastic about good creative — you genuinely appreciate well-crafted ads
- Concise commentary — let the examples speak for themselves
- Use specific language: "this hook works because..." not "this is a great ad"

## Total Word Count

**1,500–2,000 words** per page. Most of the page is visual (video cards). The writing is commentary and analysis.

## Section-by-Section Breakdown

### 1. H1 + Meta
- Format: "[Niche] UGC Video Ad Examples & Inspiration"
- Meta description: Include niche, number of examples, and a hook like "with breakdowns of why they work"
- Example: "8 skincare UGC video ad examples that actually convert — with breakdowns of the hooks, formats, and CTAs that make them work."

### 2. Opening Paragraph (60–100 words)
- Start with a specific insight about what makes ads work in this niche
- Example: "The best skincare UGC ads don't sell the product — they sell the transformation. Every high-performing skincare ad we analyzed follows the same pattern: vulnerable hook, visual proof, soft CTA. Here are 8 examples that nail it, with breakdowns of exactly why they work."
- **No generic "UGC is effective for [niche]" intros. Start with the insight.**

### 3. Why UGC Works for [Niche] (100–150 words)
- 2–3 specific reasons, not generic "UGC builds trust" statements
- Each reason should be niche-specific:
  - Skincare: "Viewers need to see the product on real skin — stock footage and studio shots don't build the same trust"
  - SaaS: "Screen recordings feel more credible than animated explainers — people want to see the actual product"
  - Food: "The sensory experience (sizzle, crunch, steam) drives cravings — UGC captures this better than polished brand content"
- Include 1 stat if available
- Keep this section SHORT. The reader came for examples, not theory.

### 4. Example Video Cards (600–800 words total for 6–8 examples)
- **This is the core of the page.** 6–8 video example cards, each with:

  **Per example (80–100 words each):**
  - **Example title** (H3): Descriptive name like "The Vulnerable Testimonial" or "The Side-by-Side Comparison"
  - **Video card**: Use the VideoCard component (the actual video placeholder — in production these would show real examples or AI-generated demos)
  - **Format tag**: [Testimonial], [Problem-Solution], [Before/After], [Unboxing], etc.
  - **Platform tag**: [TikTok], [Instagram], [Facebook], etc.
  - **Duration**: e.g., "18 seconds"
  - **Why it works** (3–4 sentences): Specific analysis of the hook, structure, and CTA
    - "The hook ('I almost returned this...') creates tension — you HAVE to keep watching to find out what happened."
    - "The mid-section shows the product in use with natural lighting, which feels authentic."
    - "The CTA is a casual 'link in bio' — no hard sell, which matches the testimonial tone."
  - **Key takeaway** (1 sentence, bold): The one thing to steal from this example
    - "**Takeaway: Start with doubt, end with conviction. The emotional arc is what keeps people watching.**"

- **Variety is critical.** The 6–8 examples should cover:
  - At least 3 different formats (testimonial, problem-solution, demo, before/after, etc.)
  - At least 2 different platforms (TikTok, Instagram, Facebook)
  - At least 2 different durations (short 15s and longer 30–45s)
  - Different hook styles (question, statement, controversy, vulnerability)

### 5. Winning Patterns Breakdown (200–300 words)
- After showing all examples, analyze the patterns across them
- Structure as 3–4 pattern observations:

  **Pattern 1: The Hook Formula**
  - "6 out of 8 examples use a personal/emotional hook in the first 2 seconds. None start with the product name or a feature."
  
  **Pattern 2: Show, Don't Tell**
  - "Every high-performing example includes a visual demonstration — the product being applied, the result being shown, the unboxing happening. Pure talking-head without visuals underperforms."
  
  **Pattern 3: Soft CTAs Win**
  - "7 out of 8 examples use soft CTAs ('link in bio', 'just try it', 'you won't regret it') rather than hard sells ('buy now', 'shop today'). The casual tone matches the UGC format."
  
  **Pattern 4: Length Sweet Spot**
  - "The best-performing examples are 15–25 seconds. Longer than 30 seconds only works for story-driven testimonials."

- **This section is what separates a good showcase page from a generic gallery.** The analysis is the value.

### 6. Create Your Own (100–150 words)
- Brief section connecting the examples to ReUGC
- "Want to create ads like these? Here's how:"
- 3 steps:
  1. "Pick a format from the examples above (testimonial and problem-solution have the highest conversion rates)"
  2. "Use the matching script template from our [template page] (link)"
  3. "Generate 5–10 variations with different hooks and test them"
- Keep it practical, not salesy. The reader should feel empowered, not pitched.

### 7. CTA Section
- Standard CTA component
- Customize: "Create [niche] video ads like these. No camera, no creators, no editing."
- Button: "Get Started"

## Formatting Rules

- Video cards should be displayed in a grid: 2 columns on desktop, 1 column on mobile
- Each video card should have the format tag and platform tag as small badges/pills
- The "Why it works" analysis should be directly below each video card, not in a separate section
- Key takeaways should be visually distinct (bold, different color, or highlighted)
- The patterns section should use a different visual treatment than the examples (e.g., numbered cards, bordered boxes)
- Link to: matching template page, matching industry page, related create pages

## What NOT to Do

- Don't show 8 examples that all look the same. Variety is the whole point
- Don't write generic analysis like "this is a great ad" — explain specifically WHY it works
- Don't skip the patterns section. Without analysis, it's just a gallery — the patterns are what make it a resource
- Don't make the "Create Your Own" section a long sales pitch. 3 steps, done
- Don't use the same "Why it works" analysis for every example. Each should highlight something different
- Don't forget platform and format tags — they help the reader find relevant examples quickly
- Don't write 500 words of intro before showing the first example. Examples first, context second
