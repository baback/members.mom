# Writing Guide: Platform Pages (`/platforms/[slug]`)

## Overview

These are high-intent pages targeting marketers who already advertise on a specific platform and want to create better video ads for it. The reader knows TikTok (or Facebook, or YouTube) — they need platform-specific tactics, specs, and scripts. Be the cheat sheet they keep open in another tab.

## Tone & Voice

- Write like a media buyer who's spent $1M+ on this platform, sharing notes
- Be extremely specific — exact pixel dimensions, exact duration limits, exact safe zones
- Tactical over theoretical. Every section should answer "what do I do?"
- Use platform-specific terminology correctly (e.g., "Spark Ads" not "sponsored posts" on TikTok)
- Confident and direct. No hedging with "it depends" — give a recommendation and explain when it doesn't apply

## Total Word Count

**2,000–2,800 words** per page. These are reference pages — dense with info, no padding.

## Section-by-Section Breakdown

### 1. H1 + Meta
- Format: "AI UGC Video Ads for [Platform]: Specs, Scripts & Strategy"
- Meta description: Include platform name, "2026 specs", and a hook like "with 3 ready-to-use scripts"
- Example: "Everything you need to create AI UGC video ads for TikTok in 2026 — specs, scripts, strategy, and what's actually working right now."

### 2. Opening Paragraph (80–120 words)
- Start with a platform-specific insight, not a generic intro
- Example: "TikTok's algorithm rewards native-looking content over polished ads. That's why UGC-style video ads consistently outperform branded content on the platform — 2.3x higher completion rates on average."
- State what this page covers: specs, formats, scripts, and strategy
- **No "TikTok is one of the most popular platforms..." — the reader already knows**

### 3. Platform Specs Table (standalone section)
- Full-width, clearly formatted table
- Must include ALL of these for every platform:
  - **Aspect ratios supported** (with recommended in bold)
  - **Video duration** (min and max, with recommended sweet spot)
  - **File size limit**
  - **File formats accepted**
  - **Resolution** (minimum and recommended)
  - **Safe zones** (text-safe area dimensions if applicable)
  - **Sound** (required/optional/recommended)
  - **Captions** (auto-generated or manual, recommended approach)
- Add a note below the table: "Specs current as of [month] 2026. Platforms update frequently — verify before launching."
- **This table alone should be worth bookmarking.** Get every number right.

### 4. Top 3 Performing Ad Formats (400–500 words)
- 3 formats that specifically work on THIS platform (not generic formats)
- Each format gets:
  - **Format name** (H3) — e.g., "The Native Testimonial" or "The Stitch-Style Reaction"
  - **Why it works on [platform]** (2–3 sentences referencing the platform's algorithm or user behavior)
  - **Structure breakdown** (visual timeline):
    - 0–3s: Hook (what happens)
    - 3–10s: Body (what happens)
    - 10–15s: CTA (what happens)
  - **Ideal length** for this format on this platform
  - **Example thumbnail/first-frame description** (what the viewer sees before pressing play)
- Formats should be DIFFERENT per platform. TikTok = native/raw. LinkedIn = professional/educational. YouTube = hook-heavy (skip button).

### 5. 3 Ready-to-Use Script Templates (500–600 words)
- 3 complete scripts, not outlines — actual words someone would say on camera
- Each script:
  - **Script name** (e.g., "The Problem-Agitate-Solve Script")
  - **Best for:** (product type, funnel stage)
  - **Duration:** (e.g., "15–20 seconds")
  - **The full script** in a code block or highlighted box:
    ```
    [HOOK — 0-3s]
    "I was spending $500/month on [product category] and nothing was working..."
    
    [BODY — 3-12s]  
    "Then I found [product]. Within 2 weeks, [specific result]."
    [Show product / demonstrate]
    
    [CTA — 12-15s]
    "Link in bio. Seriously, just try it."
    ```
  - **Why this works on [platform]** (1–2 sentences)
  - **Customization notes** (what to swap for different products)
- Scripts should feel natural and spoken, not written. Use contractions, casual language, incomplete sentences where appropriate.
- **These scripts are the #1 reason someone visits this page. Make them genuinely usable.**

### 6. Creative Testing Tips for [Platform] (200–300 words)
- 4–5 specific tips for testing video ads on this platform
- Each tip: bold headline + 2–3 sentences
- Tips should be platform-specific:
  - TikTok: "Test hooks independently — same body, 5 different hooks. TikTok's algorithm decides in the first 0.5 seconds."
  - Facebook: "Use Advantage+ Creative to let Meta auto-optimize elements. But always test UGC vs branded separately."
  - YouTube: "Your first 5 seconds determine skip rate. Test hooks with different emotional triggers."
- Include specific numbers where possible: "Test 3–5 hooks per concept", "Run each variation for at least $50 before judging"

### 7. Do's and Don'ts (150–200 words)
- Two-column layout: Do's on left, Don'ts on right
- 5–6 items per column
- Be specific to the platform:
  - TikTok DO: "Use trending sounds (even at low volume under voiceover)"
  - TikTok DON'T: "Don't use a logo watermark in the first 3 seconds — it signals 'ad' and kills watch time"
  - LinkedIn DO: "Add captions — 80% of LinkedIn video is watched on mute"
  - LinkedIn DON'T: "Don't use casual/slang tone — it feels out of place on LinkedIn"
- **Every do/don't should be something a beginner would actually get wrong**

### 8. CTA Section
- Standard CTA component
- Customize: "Create [Platform] video ads in minutes. No camera, no creators, no editing."
- Button: "Get Started"

## Formatting Rules

- Specs table must be the first major content section (people come for specs)
- Scripts should be in visually distinct boxes (code blocks, bordered cards, or shaded backgrounds)
- Timeline breakdowns (0–3s, 3–10s, etc.) should use a visual format, not just text
- Link to: matching showcase page (platform examples), related industry pages, create pages
- Add "Specs verified: [month] 2026" near the specs table

## What NOT to Do

- Don't get specs wrong. Double-check every number. Wrong specs = lost credibility instantly
- Don't write scripts that sound like blog posts read aloud. They should sound like a person talking to their phone
- Don't give the same advice for every platform. If your TikTok tips work for LinkedIn, something's wrong
- Don't skip the timeline breakdowns in the format section — that's what makes it actionable
- Don't write "post consistently" or "engage with your audience" — that's generic social media advice, not ad strategy
- Don't forget to mention sound/music strategy — it's different on every platform
